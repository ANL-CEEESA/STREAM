Model

This section provides a summary of the model stre3am. A more comprehensive description can be found under the STREAM/docs/model_d/ directory and typing make in a terminal emulator[1].

Model basic idea¶

Let us consider a single plant or asset, and let xij∈Rnvx_{ij} \in \mathbb{R}^{\mathtt{nv}}xij​∈Rnv represent a vector of measurable quantities at a particular period iii, subperiod jjj, e.g.\ the fuel consumption, or the variable O & M cost, etc. Further, assume the ordered sets P∈{1,2,… }\mathcal{P} \in \{1, 2, \dots\}P∈{1,2,…} and P1∈{1,2,… }\mathcal{P}_1 \in \{1,2,\dots\}P1​∈{1,2,…} representing the periods and subperiods. Then, assuming every quantity of the plant is related linearly to one another, the following system characterizes the asset,

Aijxij=bij  ∀i∈P,∀j∈P1,A_{ij} x_{ij} = b_{ij}\; \forall i \in \mathcal{P}, \forall j \in \mathcal{P}_1,Aij​xij​=bij​∀i∈P,∀j∈P1​,
(1)

where AijA_{ij}Aij​ is an nvar by (nv=nvar+ddd) matrix (d>0d>0d>0 degrees of freedom), and bijb_{ij}bij​ is a nvar vector of coefficients. The equation above can be used to determine the state of the plant given an arbitrary value of the ddd degrees of freedom (e.g. the active capacity).

Therefore, a plant in stre3am can have varying states according to the arbitrary values of the ddd degrees of freedom over time. A change in the technological makeup of the plant has the potential to change the relationship between state and their degrees of freedom. This is reflected in Equation (1) through the adoption characteristic matrices/vectors AijA_{ij}Aij​ and bijb_{ij}bij​ for each technology adoption in the plant. Therefore, a plant with several possible technological makeup (e.g.\ plant retrofits) has the following system,

Aijkxij=bijk  ∃k∈K,∀i∈P,∀j∈P1,A_{ijk} x_{ij} = b_{ijk}\; \exists k \in \mathcal{K}, \forall i \in \mathcal{P}, \forall j \in \mathcal{P}_1,Aijk​xij​=bijk​∃k∈K,∀i∈P,∀j∈P1​,
(2)

where K\mathcal{K}K represents the (generic) technology ordered set, which in the current implementation of stre3am can refer to retrofits or new plants. The key aspect of the problem behind stre3am is that for the plant at most one technological makeup must be true (e.g. selected) at a given time. This decision is different from the other measurable quantities insofar as its discrete nature. Let yijk∈{0,1}y_{ijk} \in \{0,1\}yijk​∈{0,1} be 1 if technology kkk is active and 0 otherwise. This binary variable is used to present the decision of a particular technology at a particular time in the following form,

Aijkνijk=bijkyijk  ∀i∈P,∀j∈P1,A_{ijk} \nu_{ijk} = b_{ijk} y_{ijk} \; \forall i \in \mathcal{P}, \forall j \in \mathcal{P}_1,Aijk​νijk​=bijk​yijk​∀i∈P,∀j∈P1​,
(3)
∑k∈Kνijk=xij, ∑k∈Kyijk=1  ∀i∈P,∀j∈P1,\sum_{k\in\mathcal{K}} \nu_{ijk} = x_{ij}, \, \sum_{k\in\mathcal{K}}y_{ijk} = 1\; \forall i \in\mathcal{P}, \forall j \in\mathcal{P}_1,k∈K∑​νijk​=xij​,k∈K∑​yijk​=1∀i∈P,∀j∈P1​,
(4)

and,

xlbyijk≤νijk≤xubyijk  ∀i∈P,∀j∈P1,x^\text{lb} y_{ijk} \leq \nu_{ijk} \leq x^\text{ub} y_{ijk} \; \forall i \in\mathcal{P}, \forall j \in\mathcal{P}_1,xlbyijk​≤νijk​≤xubyijk​∀i∈P,∀j∈P1​,
(5)

where νijk\nu_{ijk}νijk​ is the disaggregated state variable for technology kkk, and xlbx^{\text{lb}}xlb and xubx^{\text{ub}}xub are the lower- and upper-bound of the state vector. These equations link the value of yijky_{ijk}yijk​ to the state vector and the respective matrix and vector AijkA_{ijk}Aijk​ and bijkb_{ijk}bijk​. An alternative view of this system can be obtained by introducing a Boolean variable Yijk∈{True, False}Y_{ijk} \in \{\text{True, False} \}Yijk​∈{True, False} analogous to yijky_{ijk}yijk​, and the following equation,

∨‾k∈K(YijkAijkxij=bijk)  ∀i∈P,∀j∈P1,\underline{\vee}_{k\in \mathcal{K}} \begin{pmatrix} Y_{ijk} \\ A_{ijk} x_{ij} = b_{ijk} \end{pmatrix}\; \forall i \in\mathcal{P}, \forall j \in\mathcal{P}_1,∨​k∈K​(Yijk​Aijk​xij​=bijk​​)∀i∈P,∀j∈P1​,
(6)

where ∨‾\underline{\vee}∨​ represents the exclusive OR (XOR) operator. The previous equation are known as disjunctive constraints, and this particular instance assumes a single index kkk has YijkY_{ijk}Yijk​ equal True, and all else set to False. Let us assume that k=1k=1k=1 corresponds to the incumbent technology, i.e. the technology given to the plant before the first time-slice. Then, the possible values the Boolean variables over the whole horizon can be restricted through the following statements:

  • A transition to a non-incumbent technology is True once for the whole horizon.

    Yijk  ⟹  Yi,j+1,k  ∀i∈P,∀j∈P1∖{∣P1∣},∀k∈K∖{1},Y_{ijk} \implies Y_{i, j+1, k} \; \forall i \in \mathcal{P}, \forall j \in \mathcal{P}_1 \setminus \left\{|\mathcal{P}_1|\right\}, \forall k \in \mathcal{K} \setminus \left\{1\right\},Yijk​⟹Yi,j+1,k​∀i∈P,∀j∈P1​∖{∣P1​∣},∀k∈K∖{1},
    (7)

and,

Yi,∣P1∣,k  ⟹  Yi+1,1,k  ∀i∈P∖{∣P∣},∀k∈K∖{1}.Y_{i,|\mathcal{P}_1|,k} \implies Y_{i+1, 1, k} \; \forall i \in \mathcal{P} \setminus \left\{|\mathcal{P}|\right\}, \forall k \in \mathcal{K} \setminus \left\{1\right\}.Yi,∣P1​∣,k​⟹Yi+1,1,k​∀i∈P∖{∣P∣},∀k∈K∖{1}.
(8)
  • A transition from incumbent is True once for the whole horizon.
Yi,j+1,1  ⟹  Yi,j,1  ∀i∈P,∀j∈P1∖{∣P1∣},Y_{i, j+1, 1} \implies Y_{i,j, 1} \; \forall i \in \mathcal{P}, \forall j \in \mathcal{P}_1 \setminus \left\{|\mathcal{P}_1|\right\},Yi,j+1,1​⟹Yi,j,1​∀i∈P,∀j∈P1​∖{∣P1​∣},
(9)

and,

Yi+1,1,1  ⟹  Yi,∣P1∣,1  ∀i∈P∖{∣P∣}.Y_{i+1, 1, 1} \implies Y_{i,|\mathcal{P}_1|,1} \; \forall i \in \mathcal{P} \setminus \left\{|\mathcal{P}|\right\}.Yi+1,1,1​⟹Yi,∣P1​∣,1​∀i∈P∖{∣P∣}.
(10)

These logic propositions collectively written here as Ω(Y)=True\Omega(Y) = \text{True}Ω(Y)=True. These propositions can also be written in terms of binary variables and algebraic equations, viz.,

yijk−yi,j+1,k≤0  ∀i∈P,∀j∈P1∖{∣P1∣},∀k∈K∖{1},y_{ijk} - y_{i,j+1,k} \leq 0 \; \forall i \in \mathcal{P}, \forall j \in \mathcal{P}_1 \setminus \left\{|\mathcal{P}_1|\right\}, \forall k \in \mathcal{K} \setminus \left\{1\right\},yijk​−yi,j+1,k​≤0∀i∈P,∀j∈P1​∖{∣P1​∣},∀k∈K∖{1},
(11)
yi,∣P1∣,k−yi+1,1,k≤0  ∀i∈P∖{∣P∣},∀k∈K∖{1},y_{i,|\mathcal{P}_1|,k} - y_{i+1,1,k} \leq 0 \; \forall i \in \mathcal{P} \setminus \left\{|\mathcal{P}|\right\}, \forall k \in \mathcal{K} \setminus \left\{1\right\},yi,∣P1​∣,k​−yi+1,1,k​≤0∀i∈P∖{∣P∣},∀k∈K∖{1},
(12)
yi,j+1,1−yij,1≤0  ∀i∈P,∀j∈P1∖{∣P1∣},y_{i,j+1,1} - y_{ij,1} \leq 0 \; \forall i \in \mathcal{P}, \forall j \in \mathcal{P}_1 \setminus \left\{|\mathcal{P}_1|\right\},yi,j+1,1​−yij,1​≤0∀i∈P,∀j∈P1​∖{∣P1​∣},
(13)
yi+1,1,1−yi,∣P1∣,1≤0  ∀i∈P∖{∣P∣}.y_{i+1,1,1} - y_{i,|\mathcal{P}_1|,1} \leq 0 \; \forall i \in \mathcal{P} \setminus \left\{|\mathcal{P}|\right\}.yi+1,1,1​−yi,∣P1​∣,1​≤0∀i∈P∖{∣P∣}.
(14)

Which collectively are written here as:

Hyijk≥h  ∀i∈P,∀j∈P1,∀k∈K.H y_{ijk} \geq h \; \forall i \in \mathcal{P}, \forall j \in \mathcal{P}_1, \forall k \in \mathcal{K}.Hyijk​≥h∀i∈P,∀j∈P1​,∀k∈K.
(15)

Objective function¶

The model uses the minimization of the Net Present Value (NPV) as objective function. Nevertheless, users can define custom objective functions in the same way as any JuMP model. The NPV in stre3am has several components, most noticeable the discount factor δij\delta_{ij}δij​, which can be calculated from the assumed interest rate and year. The objective function considers the following items for every period and subperiod tuple ((i,j)∈P×P1(i,j)\in\mathcal{P}\times\mathcal{P}_1(i,j)∈P×P1​),

  1. Capital cost installments (CI)
  2. Fixed operations and maintenance (fO&M)
  3. Variable operations and maintenance (vO&M)
  4. Fuel cost (FC)
  5. Electricity cost (EC)
  6. Feedstock cost (FdC)
  7. CCS cost (CCSC)
  8. Plant retirement (PR)
  9. Last period loan factor (LLF).
min⁡∑i∈P∑j∈P1δijCIij+δijfO&Mij+δijvO&Mij+δijFCij+δijECij+δijFdCij+δijCCSCij+δijPRij+LLF.\begin{align*} \min \sum_{i\in\mathcal{P}} \sum_{j\in\mathcal{P}_1} & \delta_{ij} \text{CI}_{ij} + \delta_{ij} \text{fO\&M}_{ij} + \delta_{ij}\text{vO\&M}_{ij} + \delta_{ij}\text{FC}_{ij} \\ + & \delta_{ij}\text{EC}_{ij} + \delta_{ij}\text{FdC}_{ij} + \delta_{ij}\text{CCSC}_{ij} + \delta_{ij}\text{PR}_{ij} \\ + & \text{LLF}. \end{align*}mini∈P∑​j∈P1​∑​++​δij​CIij​+δij​fO&Mij​+δij​vO&Mij​+δij​FCij​δij​ECij​+δij​FdCij​+δij​CCSCij​+δij​PRij​LLF.​
(16)

The terms in the previous equation are related to the rest of variables from the model linearly.

As mentioned in the model basis section, the model can be expressed either with disjunctions, logic statements, and Boolean (and continuous) variables[2], or with algebraic equations and binary variables, i.e. MILP.
